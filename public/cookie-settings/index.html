Current Cookie and Storage Position

What the public site is currently designed to do.

No intentional non-essential cookies in public code

Based on the current public site implementation, AI Syndicate is not intentionally deploying client-side advertising pixels, marketing trackers, or embedded analytics scripts that would ordinarily trigger a full consumer-facing cookie preference center.

Infrastructure may still process technical data

As with most websites, hosting, CDN, firewall, security, or load-balancing infrastructure may still process ordinary technical request information needed to serve, secure, and monitor the site.

No promise beyond the live configuration

This page describes the site’s present posture. If AI Syndicate later adds analytics, advertising, personalization, or other non-essential tracking tools, this page should be updated to reflect the actual implementation.

No “sale” or “sharing” opt-out flow today

Because the public site is not intentionally configured to sell personal information or share it for cross-context behavioral advertising, AI Syndicate does not currently present a separate sale/sharing opt-out interface on the public site.

Your Controls

How visitors can control cookies and browser storage now.

Browser and device settings

You can generally review, block, restrict, or delete cookies and browser storage through your browser or device privacy settings, including in Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge.

Do Not Track and similar signals

The current public site is not configured to change behavior based on browser “Do Not Track” signals, and it is not intentionally configured for the type of cross-site behavioral advertising that would typically rely on such signals.

If The Site Changes

What AI Syndicate should do before adding non-essential tracking.

Update this page

If analytics, advertising, personalization, A/B testing, or other non-essential tools are added, this page should be updated to describe those tools and the categories of data involved.

Update the Privacy Policy

Any change in data collection, disclosure, retention, sale/sharing, or cross-site tracking posture should also be reflected in the Privacy Policy.

Add consent or opt-out tools where required

If non-essential cookies or tracking technologies are introduced in a way that triggers consent or opt-out obligations under applicable law, AI Syndicate should implement the corresponding consent or preference controls before or at deployment.

Keep claims accurate

Privacy claims, cookie disclosures, and any “settings” or “preferences” interface should describe the site’s real technical behavior, not an aspirational or copied template.
